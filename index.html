Hello, my name is Hannah Turner, a user experience designer based in Atlanta, Georgia. I graduated from Columbus State University in 2019 with a B.S. in Computer Science and soon after completed the UX/UI Design Bootcamp offered by Georgia Tech in 2020. I am passionate about combining my technology and programming background with my love for design to create beautiful, clean user experiences. I specialize in app and website design, and I thrive when presented with challenging user problems.



My skillset includes:

UX Research

When presented with a problem or goal, I first sit down and brainstorm a list of questions I have for the user regarding that goal. I then translate those questions into an actionable interview script and perform 5-10 user interviews (more if needed). Once a substantial amount of insight is provided, I organize the answers into an affinity diagram to prioritize user needs. If more research is required, I will create a brief survey to distribute among the target audience and revisit the affinity diagram with results.

Wireframe/Prototype Design

I typically begin my wireframing process by using paper and pencil to sketch out my original idea. If working with a team, I find that physically sketching the wireframe allows for collaboration and easy editing. Once a solid sketch is produced, I then translate the wireframe onto Adobe XD and prototype the user flow one page at a time. Once a solid wireframe and prototype is created, guerilla testing can be performed to reveal any dead ends or flaws in the design.

User Testing

It is important to keep in mind that I am the designer, not the user. A concept or layout that I find appealing may be confusing or unappealing to the end user. To ensure the user’s needs are being addressed throughout the design process, I conduct continuous user testing. Whether I give someone with a 5-second test to gain a general opinion, or conduct a full 20-minute test navigating a website prototype, information gained from these tests always ensure improvement and user satisfaction.
